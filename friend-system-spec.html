Friends on your laptop — design spec 🐱🏠

Interrogating the foundations: why a Room? 1:1 or group? why a Deck? — and what to build instead.

Headline: six independent analyses converged on the same answer — the Room and the Deck are both the wrong primitives. The real primitive is a mutual 1:1 friendship; your friends are always present on your desktop; the loop is a reciprocal poke (which we removed and should bring back). Because the schema is already 1:1 under the hood, this is mostly a teardown, not a build.

1 · The founder's questions, answered crisply

Why do we need a "Room" at all? Is it the right primitive?

No — kill it. A Room is "a place anyone with the code can enter," but the product is "a toy for people who already like each other." That demands consent, and consent lives on a friendship edge, not a shared password. Three concrete failures prove it never earned its weight:

  • The default PATPARTY code drops a couple — your primary user — into a channel with strangers. That's the anti-intimacy failure mode.
  • "Extra Rooms = Pro" taxes a basic need (having two friend groups) behind the paywall.
  • Room-granular presence can't express "around for my partner, away for my team."
  • And the code confirms it: join_room_by_code grants membership by possession of a string, and profiles_read is global — the Room is a leaky boundary, not a trust boundary.

The wireframes' own verdict: the Room is "purely a staging area" with no interactions of its own. A primitive whose only job is to feed another primitive is a smell.

Is communication 1:1, group, or broadcast?

Presence is symmetric many-to-many within your friends ("my people are around" — and if I can see you, you can see me; never silent one-way lurking). Interaction is always directed 1:1 ("I miss a person; I poke that person"). No broadcast, no "poke the room." This is what makes the model identical at n=2 and n=15 — scaling only adds more passive moods to glance at; the active signal never turns into a noisy group chat.

Why do we need a "Deck"/dock? Is bringing friends onto the desktop right?

No — kill it as a gate. The Deck splits presence (Room) from interaction (Deck), then gates all interaction on a staging step. The wireframe matrix is damning: in "Both IN" — the most common resting state — zero interaction is possible. At n=2, a Room window holding one person plus a separate Deck is absurd overhead. Friends should be always present the moment they're online; "which friends are drawn right now" becomes a purely local, cosmetic choice — never a network or consent act.

…and the other concepts?
  • Invite codes → replace with a one-tap invite link that mints a single friendship edge to you on accept (reusable for seeding a crew; each accept = one edge). Capability-style "add a person," not "join a channel."
  • Character-as-self → keep it. Picking your pixel avatar is the Tamagotchi/Memoji hook — the one questioned concept that survives untouched.
  • Pat (local-only) → fix it. A pat that never reaches your friend is "affection that is theater." Make it a real per-edge event that reaches them.

3 · The recommended model (A) — and why

A mutual 1:1 friend graph; friends always present on a slim edge-anchored shelf; a reciprocal poke as the loop.

  • Primitive = friendship. friendships(a_uid, b_uid, accepted) replaces room_members. A couple is "one edge." A crew is "everyone added each other." No primitive switch as you scale.
  • Discovery = a one-tap invite link (digital-pat://add?token=…) that creates a single edge to you on accept. Tokens are single-use, expiring, uid-bound, unguessable, with an explicit multi-use revocable option for seeding a crew. The easy path is never the unsafe path.
  • Presence = symmetric, per-edge. Each client owns one channel presence:<uid> carrying only {charId, mood, lastActive}; accepted friends subscribe (RLS-gated). A prominent per-friend "appear away" makes it opt-out, never silent. Payload stays category-mood only — never app/URL/window/keys.
  • Surface = always-on shelf. Friends' pets live on a slim, edge-anchored, auto-hideable "windowsill" — always present, no staging. One representation per friend, always live-mood, always pokeable. Escape hatch: drag a pet off the shelf to let it roam free (the old playful free-floating delight, now opt-in instead of a precondition).
  • Interaction = the poke, brought back. Re-wire the already-built send_poke → pokes → Postgres-changes path, swapping the gate from is_member to is_friend. Restores the daily loop + keeps free streaks / who-missed-you. Make "pat" a real event too: tapping a friend's pet sends affection that reaches them (hop + hearts on both screens, with local sender feedback) — fixing the three documented wireframe gaps.
┌─ my desktop ───────────────────────────────┐ │ [my work] │ │ │ │ ░░ windowsill shelf (auto-hide) ░░░░░░░░░░░ │ │ 🐱 🐰coding 🐼vibing 🐹away │ ← friends, always there, │ me Ava Ven Sid │ live mood, tap=pat, ⌥=poke └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘ n=2 → the shelf just has you + one friend. No room. No deck. No code dance.

Why A over B/C: B fails its own n=2 wedge (dead "Both IN" state, strangers-by-default, the live privacy leak) — by removing pokes, the shipped product iterated itself back into the low-frequency presence graveyard it set out to avoid. C is emotionally purest but discards the crew market and the assets already built (the 22 characters are the founder's crew). A serves both with one primitive, fixes the privacy hole, removes the Pro tax on a basic need, and is nearly free to build because the schema is already 1:1.

The one hard discipline: if you later add "crews," keep them a dumb personal saved-set lens over your own edges (group ⊆ accepted-friends) — no join/leave/ownership/code. The moment a group gains those, it silently becomes a Room and you've rebuilt the overhead you just removed. Personal-groups first; promote to shared only on real demand; defer entirely past v-next.

4 · Keep / Kill / Change vs today

KILL

  • The Room as a first-class concept (channel, roster window, join-by-code, room_members, "extra Rooms = Pro").
  • The PATPARTY default code + the shared-code security model.
  • The Deck as an interaction gate ("take out / send back" before you can pat or poke).
  • Local-only pat (a no-op pretending to be affection) and the recipient-deck-dependent poke landing (illegible).

CHANGE

  • room_members → friendships edge table; send_poke's gate → is_friend.
  • Presence keyed by room → keyed by publisher (presence:<uid>, friends subscribe RLS-gated).
  • Invite code → single-use, expiring, uid-bound invite link (+ optional revocable multi-use).
  • profiles_read using(true) → friend-scoped — ship this immediately (live leak).
  • "Appear away" from a paint-toggle → a real data-layer stop of presence broadcast (per friend).
  • Monetization re-based fully on AI-generation COGS + cosmetics; the "extra Rooms" SKU disappears.

KEEP

  • Character-as-self (22 predefined + generate-your-own) — the hook, untouched.
  • The pokes table, server rate-limit, blocks, streaks/who-missed-you — already built, just re-pointed at friendships.
  • Anonymous auth + Keychain identity (magic-link as the durable-identity backstop later).
  • Category-mood-only presence payload + non-activating overlay etiquette.

5 · Phased build plan

v2.3 — the teardown + the loop (v-next minimal)

  1. Hotfix now (independent): patch profiles_read to friend-scoped. (Live leak.)
  2. friendships + friend_requests tables; request/accept RPCs; swap send_poke gate to is_friend.
  3. Per-user presence channel presence:<uid> + RLS subscription auth; client RoomStore → FriendStore with subscribe-all / teardown-all on sleep/wake.
  4. Always-on shelf replaces the Room window + Deck; one live-mood pet per online friend; menu-bar roster popover for overflow; density cap.
  5. Bring back the poke (re-wire existing path) + make pat a real per-edge event with both-sided feedback. Keep streaks/who-missed-you.
  6. One-tap invite link (single-use, expiring, uid-bound) + deep-link accept.
  7. Migration: convert each existing room's co-members into mutual friendships (one-time backfill) or an in-app "re-add your people" prompt — messaged as a privacy upgrade, not a regression.
  8. Prominent per-friend "appear away" (real broadcast stop) + one-tap block.

v2.4 — fast-follow

  • Cross-member custom-sprite sync via Supabase Storage (RLS keyed on friendship; payload never carries sprite bytes; orphan cleanup on edge removal).
  • Describe-to-create at volume + credit packs; cosmetics/themes; custom poke phrases (author-time presets, never live free text).
  • Magic-link cross-device merge (durable identity for block-as-safety).

Later (only on real demand)

  • Personal "crew" groups — a dumb saved-set lens over edges. Hold the group ⊆ friends invariant in schema. Promote to shared objects only if users genuinely need a shared roster.

6 · Open decisions for you (with my lean)

1. Crew of 5–15: real target or n=2 only? Sets how much shelf-density/overflow to build. Lean: keep crew secondary — the friend graph serves it for free, so don't pay for it upfront.
2. Crew "groups": personal lenses or shared objects? The single call that determines whether you truly escaped the Room. Lean: personal-first; escalate only on demand.
3. Poke to an offline friend: deliver on next launch ("X poked you while away") or silently drop? Biggest data-model fork. Lean: log to pokes for streak math, but never surface as a chat history — ephemeral in the moment, persistent only for the loop.
4. Pat vs poke: two affordances or one? Lean: poke = directed nudge that reaches them; pat = softer affection that also transmits (hop + hearts on their pet). Both real; neither local-only.
5. "Appear away": global or per-friend? Lean: per-friend (the graph's native strength), default-visible to friends.
6. Identity durability before launch: require magic-link before block-as-safety, or accept reinstall-orphans until v2.4? Lean: ship anon + mutual-accept as the re-entry barrier now; magic-link later.
7. Friend-count ceiling + graceful degradation for presence fan-out (e.g. subscribe only to pinned/on-screen friends past N). Set the cap explicitly.

7 · The six lenses (each reached the same place)

Social unit

Room vs 1:1 edge vs friend-graph?
→ Friend graph; a "group" is a thin personal lens, not a channel. The Room is borrowed scaffolding hiding a 1:1 graph the schema already implements.

Interaction surface

Why a Deck/dock?
→ Always-present on a slim edge-anchored shelf (one representation per friend), drag-off to roam as opt-in delight. Delete the Room-then-Deck staging ritual.

Communication topology

1:1, group, or broadcast?
→ Symmetric presence + directed 1:1 poke/message. Three payloads only: mood (passive), poke (nudge), message (poke + tiny preset/emoji). No threads, no inbox.

Consent / privacy

Who sees whom, on whose terms?
→ Mutual-consent friend graph; single-use expiring uid-bound invites; fix the global profiles_read leak now; "appear away" as a real broadcast stop.

Prior art

How does the genre solve this?
→ Locket/BeReal/Find My all use mutual-accept friend graphs + always-on presence + a light reciprocal signal — never a code-joined room, never a manual staging dock.

Tech architecture

Simplest correct backend?
→ friendships + friend_requests; one presence:<uid> channel per user that friends subscribe to (RLS-authorized); keep pokes/blocks/usage_counters; custom sprites via Storage later.
