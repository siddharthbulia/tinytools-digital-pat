PRD · app-factory · v1

Digital Pat 🐱

A tiny kitten that lives on your screen, reacts to whatever you're doing, and tells you where your day went — without ever reading your content.

macOS · Swift + AppKit Floating desktop pet Zero permissions · zero network Free: the pet + today view Pro: $14.99 one-time
Digital Pat is a tiny kitten that lives in the top-right corner of your Mac screen and quietly reacts to whatever you're doing. Open Chrome and it gets to work; jump on Zoom and it throws on a little blazer; fire up Claude or a terminal and it puts on glasses to "study." It never reads your content — it only knows which app is in front — and at the end of the day it shows you a cute breakdown of where your hours went. You can pat it anytime and it'll purr and smile back (you pat Pat). It's an ambient, low-stakes companion for people who spend all day at a laptop and want a friendlier screen.

01

Problem

People who work on a laptop all day stare at a focused, joyless screen for 8–10 hours. Two small, real pains stack up:

  1. The screen is lonely and sterile. There's no warmth, no companion, nothing that makes the workday feel a little less like a void. Gen-Z especially decorates everything (phone, desktop, Notion) for vibes — the Mac desktop is the last un-decorated surface.
  2. Nobody actually knows where their day went. "I was busy all day" but doing what? Time-tracking tools (RescueTime, Timing) exist but they feel like surveillance dashboards — heavy, guilt-inducing, enterprise-flavored. Nobody opens them for fun.
Concrete moment: It's 6pm. Aanya, a 24-year-old product designer, closes her laptop and has no idea whether she spent the day in Figma or doom-scrolling Slack. She'd never install a "productivity tracker" — that sounds like a chore — but she would keep a cute kitten on her screen that happened to tell her, at the end of the day, "you spent 4h in design mode 🎨 and 2h in meetings 👔."

02

Target user

Aanya, 24, product designer at a startup. Lives on her MacBook. Has a curated desktop wallpaper, Spotify always open, decorates her Notion with emojis. Uses Figma, Chrome, Slack, Zoom, Notion all day. She's tried a time tracker once, found it clinical and guilt-trippy, deleted it in a week. She likes Tamagotchi nostalgia, desktop pets, and lo-fi "study with me" streams. She wants her screen to feel alive, and she'd love a gentle, non-judgmental mirror of her day.

Anti-personas (NOT building for)

  • The serious productivity optimizer who wants billable-hour reports, Pomodoro enforcement, and CSV exports for their manager. (That's RescueTime. We are the opposite vibe.)
  • Privacy-hostile use cases — we will never read screen content, keystrokes, or window titles. If a feature needs that, it's out.
  • Windows/Linux users (v1 is Mac-only).

03

The 30-second pitch

A cute kitten lives in the corner of your screen and reacts to whatever app you're using — coding, in meetings, browsing, writing. Pat it when you need a friend, and at the end of the day it shows you a sweet little summary of where your time went.

04

Wedge feature

The kitten reacts, live, to your active app — and it's adorable. Within 30 seconds of installing, you switch from Chrome to Zoom and watch the kitten swap into a tiny blazer. That single "omg it noticed" moment is the entire hook. Everything else (summaries, history, skins) is bonus.

05

What's in the box

🆓 Free — fully usable forever

  • The pet itself. A kitten floating on top of all windows, top-right. Frameless, transparent, always-on-top, click-to-pat.
  • Live activity reactions. Detects the frontmost app and switches into the matching mood state (sprite + outfit + animation + caption).
  • Pat interaction. Click → purr, smile, floating hearts, a one-line caption.
  • Today view. Total active time + breakdown by mood category, cute labels. Resets at midnight.
  • Drag to reposition — remembers where you put it.
  • Launch at login — on by default, one toggle off.
  • Quiet/hide toggle — hotkey + tray item; keeps logging quietly.
  • Zero permissions, zero network. Works instantly. Nothing leaves your machine.

⭐ Pro — for the daily user $14.99 one-time

  1. History & recaps. 7/30-day views, a weekly recap card ("this week: 18h coding, most 🐱 day was Tuesday"), calendar heatmap. The natural upgrade trigger — you love today, then want yesterday.
  2. The wardrobe. Alternate breeds/skins (orange tabby, calico, black cat, Siamese, + a puppy / red panda) and outfit packs (cozy hoodie, pixel/Y2K, sleep cap, party).
  3. Custom moods. Map your own apps to moods and edit captions ("Linear open → 'shipping mode 🚢'").

Why one-time: a delight/vibe purchase, not a utility-ROI one. A subscription would feel gross and kill conversion. Impulse-priced like a phone-case skin pack; same band as other tinytools Pro tiers.

Out of scope (explicitly)

No reading screen content / titles / URLs / keystrokes — ever No meditation / breaks / Pomodoro No notifications / streaks / guilt No sync / accounts / leaderboards No AI captions in v1 No Windows / Linux

06

UX

Where it lives & how it behaves

⚠️ One intentional break from the factory norm: this is a floating desktop pet, not a menu-bar popup. It keeps a tray icon for the boring stuff, but the pet is the star.
  • A small frameless, transparent, always-on-top window (~120×120pt) anchored top-right by default, draggable, position remembered.
  • A menu-bar tray icon (🐱) for: open Today/recap, hide/show pet, settings, quit, upgrade.
  • Click-through everywhere except the kitten — only its body is interactive (pat / drag).
  • First launch: kitten fades in, waves a paw, one-time bubble: "hi! i'm Pat 🐱 i'll hang out while you work. pat me anytime!" No setup, no wizard, no permission prompts.

Interactions

  • Pat (single click): purr + ears perk + floating hearts + canned caption. Tiny cooldown so mashing doesn't spam.
  • Drag: reposition; loose edge-snapping; remembers spot.
  • Hover: current-mood speech bubble for ~2s.
  • Click bubble / tray → Today: the small summary card.
  • Hide: hotkey (default ⌥⌘P) or tray toggle → fades out, keeps logging.

Unhappy paths

  • Can't read frontmost app (shouldn't happen) → default kitten, keep running.
  • Multiple displays → stays on remembered display; if gone, reset to primary top-right.
  • Fullscreen / Space change → follows the active Space, stays on top.
  • Free user opens history → friendly upsell card ("Pat only remembers today on free — unlock history ✨").

The sprites / mood states — the heart of the app

Art direction: soft, round, chibi kitten. Gen-Z / cozy-funky. Big head, tiny body, huge sparkly eyes, little triangle ears, stubby paws, an expressive tail. Thick outlines, pastel accents, squishy proportions, subtle loop animations (breathing, blinking, ear-twitch, tail-flick) so it never looks frozen.

08

Tech sketch

  • Stack: Native Swift + AppKit — best for a borderless always-on-top transparent window, sprite animation, low memory, tiny binary. Window-mode app: borderless floating panel and a menu-bar status item, but no Dock icon (overrides the template's LSUIElement norm).
  • Activity detection: NSWorkspace.shared.frontmostApplication + didActivateApplicationNotification → active app's bundle id & name. No Accessibility, no Screen Recording, no Full Disk Access. Idle via CGEventSourceSecondsSinceLastEventType (no input-monitoring permission).
  • App → mood mapping: a static bundle-ID → mood dictionary shipped in the app (com.google.Chrome → Browsing). Pro custom overrides stored locally. Unknown ids → default kitten.
  • Sprite rendering: sprite-sheet PNGs, frame-by-frame in a layer-backed view (or tiny SpriteKit scene). No web view, no Electron. Low FPS for idle loops.
  • Persistence: local only. Today's tally in memory + small JSON/SQLite in ~/Library/Application Support/DigitalPat/. Pro history = retaining the file across days + aggregation. Position/settings in UserDefaults.
  • Background work: lightweight event-driven loop accumulating seconds per mood whenever the app is open (= always). Negligible CPU at idle.
  • License-gating: locally-validated signed key (no network roundtrip), gating history/recaps, wardrobe, custom moods. Same pattern as other Pro apps in the factory.
  • Permissions needed: none beyond default. (A selling point: "no scary permission prompts.")

09

Success metrics

  • I keep Pat on my own screen for 3 weeks straight without hiding/uninstalling out of annoyance. (The real dummy test for an ambient app: does it survive the irritation threshold?)
  • The pat interaction gets used unprompted — ≥ 3×/day without being told to. Signals companion, not wallpaper.
  • 5 friends install it; ≥ 2 still running after a week; ≥ 1 buys Pro (almost certainly for the wardrobe).
  • Lands on r/macapps or design Twitter/TikTok with a "this is so cute" reaction — virality is the GTM; a clip of the kitten swapping outfits is the ad.

10

Risks

1
Annoyance / occlusion. An always-on-top corner thing can cover UI and get irritating fast. Mitigations: small footprint, click-through except the body, instant hide hotkey, draggable, edge-snapping, low-key idle motion. Kill criterion: if I reach for hide more than a couple times a day in week 1, the default behavior is wrong.
2
"It's a toy, not worth paying for." Cuteness alone may not convert to $15. Mitigation: the wardrobe is a proven cosmetic-purchase pattern for this audience; history/recaps add a keep-reason. If conversion is dead, the lever is more/better skins, not more "productivity" features (which would betray the vibe).
3
Art quality is the whole product. If the kitten looks like generic AI slop or animations are stiff, the wedge fails and nothing else matters. Mitigation: treat sprite art as the #1 deliverable; get the base kitten + 3 hero moods (coding, meeting, idle) genuinely charming before breadth. Kill criterion: if the hero sprites don't make a test user smile on sight, stop and redo the art.

11

Non-goals & explicit cuts

  • No AI commentary in v1. Captions are a curated, hand-written canned set per mood. LLM quips are a tempting v2, but v1 must be fully local, offline, zero-cost, instant — and canned lines are funnier hand-written anyway.
  • No wellness/meditation/nudge layer. Explicitly cut. Pat observes and vibes; it does not coach.
  • No content awareness. Never what's on screen, only which app. A permanent product principle — the trust foundation.
  • No sync/accounts. Local-only keeps it private, simple, backend-free. Revisit only if multi-device demand is real.
