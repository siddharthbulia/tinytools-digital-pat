The whole idea in one breath

You and your people share a Room. Everyone picks a little pixel character. From then on, each friend's character lives right on your desktop — wandering in the corner, quietly changing as their day changes. Miss someone? Poke them, and their pal does a happy hop with a little message. That's it. It's presence you can feel and the cutest, lowest-effort way to say hi, thinking of you.

How it feels

🏠
They live on your screen

Each friend in your Room shows up as a little character on your desktop — not tucked in a window. Your crew, hanging out at the edge of your day.

🪄
They change with their day

Someone starts coding → glasses. Hops into a meeting → blazer. Vibing → headphones. It updates live on your side, the moment theirs changes.

👋
Poke to say hi

Tap a friend → their pal jumps with a bubble ("miss you 💕"). By default it's a poke; or send a tiny "yo", "wassuppp", or an emoji.

The poke, precisely 🐾

A poke animates the character itself — not a separate popup:

  • If that friend is already on your screen → their pal starts jumping right where it is, with the message in a bubble above it.
  • If they're not on screen → their pal pops into the top-right and jumps there with the message, then settles in.

It never steals focus or covers what you're doing — the pals float gently over the desktop.

What changed from the first cut

v2.0 showed the Room as a roster window you opened. This revision makes it ambient: the friends are on the desktop itself, the poke animates the character in place (or top-right if absent), and everyone's avatar + mood syncs live. Also fixing: the roster emptying out after a poke.

Cute, never creepy 🤍

Your friends only ever see your mood category (coding / vibing / meeting / napping…) — never your app names, your URLs, or what you type. There's an "appear away" toggle, and your own solo pet keeps working even offline. No dashboards, no scores, no surveillance — just affection.

Under the hood

Native Swift. Supabase Realtime Presence carries each person's {name, character, mood}; pokes go through a gated send_poke and arrive via Postgres Changes. Each remote friend renders as a small non-activating, click-through floating panel — so they live on your desktop without ever getting in your way.
