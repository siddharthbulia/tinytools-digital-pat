Digital Pat — 2-person scenarios 🐱🐰

Every combination of who's "in the Room" vs "on the Deck", with pat and poke — your screen vs your friend's screen, and exactly what today's code does.

The model. You = 🐱 (Me), friend = 🐰 (Bob). Each side independently decides whether the other is IN (just sitting in the Room window) or OUT (pulled onto the desktop / "deck").
• You can only pat / poke a friend who's on YOUR deck (the Room itself has no interactions — tapping a tile just takes them out).
• PAT = click their deck pet → 💗 + hop. Local only in the current build (does not reach them).
• POKE = right-click → sends over the network. How it lands on their screen depends on whether you are on their deck.

What the wireframes reveal (the honest gaps)

1. Pat never reaches your friend. It's a local animation only. In all of B2 / C2 / D3, the other person sees nothing. Likely wrong if a pat is meant as affection you send.
2. No feedback when YOU poke. Your own screen does nothing — you can't tell the poke sent (B3 / C1 / D2).
3. Poke landing (in-place vs top-right) is decided by the recipient's deck, not yours. You poke the same way; whether your pet appears in-place or pops top-right on their screen depends on whether they took you out. Non-obvious but arguably fine.
4. You can't pat/poke someone you haven't taken out. Intended ("play only when out"), but it means the Room is purely a staging area.
