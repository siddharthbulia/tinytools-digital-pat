What changed in rev. 2 (after the two-reviewer gate):
  • Reframed around poke + pet, not "presence" — presence alone is the low-frequency trap that killed Sneek / Sqwiggle / Tandem.
  • Works for 2 (a couple / a duo) as well as a crew — a Room of 2 beats cold-start and the frequency problem (the Locket lesson).
  • No subscription — generous free + $14.99 one-time + optional AI credit packs.
  • Streaks & "who-missed-you" are FREE (the retention loop); your one invite-coded Room is free & private.
  • Split into a thin, hardened v2.0 and a v2.1 fast-follow; engineering blockers promoted to hard requirements.

1 · Problem

Far-apart friends, couples, and remote crews lose the ambient "we're here together" feeling. Tools are too heavy (a Slack message is a task, a call is an event) or too cold (a green dot, "last seen"). There's no warm, zero-effort way to feel a person nearby and give them a little nudge — no words, no pressure.

v1 already gives one person a companion that reacts to what they're doing. The insight: make it two-player. Your buddy lives on your friend's desktop and theirs on yours; a glance says they're around; a poke is the lightest way to talk.

2 · Target user

Two shapes of the same simple thing — a Room you share with people you already like:

  • A pair (the cold-start-proof wedge): a long-distance couple, best friends, two co-founders. Two people is enough to be magical and high-frequency.
  • A small crew of 5–15 already online together: a remote startup, a friend group, a Discord clique. (Literally the founder's own team — the 22 bundled characters are them.)

Not for: large public communities, strangers, serious chat. A toy for people who already like each other.

3 · The 30-second pitch

Pick your pixel character (or make one from a selfie — or just describe one). Share a Room code with your person or your crew. Their little buddies now live in a cozy corner of your screen, quietly shifting as people code, vibe, or slip into meetings. Miss someone? Poke them — your character hops onto their screen and bounces around going "miss you 💕". Poke back. The lowest-effort, highest-cute way to say hi, thinking of you.

4 · Wedge & upgrade trigger

  • Wedge (free forever): "poke your person." Their pixel self on your desktop + a cute poke. Works with just two people — no empty-room problem.
  • Upgrade trigger (Pro): the moment you want to make characters on demand — describe "a grumpy wizard cat" and watch it appear. The irresistible, shareable part — and the part that costs real OpenAI money. Free taste; volume is paid credits.

5 · What's in the box

FREE  Generous, fun forever

  • Your Room, private & free — create/join one invite-coded Room (great for a pair or a crew).
  • All 22 predefined characters as your avatar.
  • 1 custom character from a photo + 1 free "describe-it" generation (a taste of the magic).
  • Live presence: your avatar auto-mirrors your activity mood; see everyone's in real time.
  • Unlimited pokes + preset micro-messages ("yo", "miss you", "wassuppp", "gm", "🫶", "👀", "🎉") + emoji.
  • Streaks & "who missed you" — poke history + day-streaks. (the retention loop — free)
  • A local library of unlimited characters; switch your active one anytime.

PRO  "Pat Pro" — $14.99 one-time + AI credit packs (no subscription)

  • Describe-to-create at volume + unlimited custom characters — via credit packs (~$4.99 / 1,000 credits). (direct OpenAI COGS)
  • Extra / multiple Rooms beyond your free one.
  • Custom poke phrases & seasonal emoji packs.
  • Cosmetics — hats/accessories, Room backdrops/themes.
v1's own (correct) reasoning: recurring billing "feels gross and kills conversion" for a vibe purchase. So Pro is a one-time unlock; the only recurring spend is optional AI credits that map 1:1 to our cost.

Out of scope (explicit)

Real chat / threads / DMs / long messages · voice / video / screenshare · mobile · public discovery / strangers / global feed · file sharing · typing indicators / read receipts · any manager / surveillance / leaderboard framing.

6 · UX

6.1 The poke — the hero loop

  • Default = poke. One tap → their app spawns your character, which hops in and bounces with "👋" for a few seconds, then leaves.
  • Or a micro-message: preset / emoji → same cute invasion with your line in the bubble. Poke back in one tap.
  • Cute: poke combos, "poked!" confetti, gentle mutable sound.
  • Etiquette (hard rule): a non-activating overlay that never steals keyboard focus or interrupts typing.

6.2 The Room — the cozy backdrop

A small always-available pixel space where each member's companion mills about in its current mood pose (coding/vibing/meeting/browsing/creating/idle) with a name tag. Glance → read the room. Draggable window + menu-bar peek.

6.3 Joining & identity

First launch → name + avatar → create/join a Room by code (sharing a code is the whole onboarding). Identity persistence (hard req): Supabase anonymous auth, refresh token in Keychain; optional email magic-link to carry/merge identity across machines; reinstall behavior defined.

6.4 Presence sync — passive, cute-not-creepy

Publishes only {name, characterId, mood, lastActiveAt}. Never the app name, window title, URL, or keystrokes — only the category mood. Plus an "appear away" toggle. No dashboards, ever. Reliability (hard req): reconnect, sleep/wake re-subscribe, stale-ghost timeout.

6.5 Character creation

Predefined (instant, free) · from a photo (free: 1) · from a description (free: 1 taste, then credits). All via the Supabase Edge Function (key server-side).

7 · Tech sketch

  • Backend = existing Supabase project digital-pat (cpfbipbdsokoshzgqvsr).
  • Client = official supabase-swift SDK → native Realtime Presence (roster) + Broadcast (pokes). No hand-rolled websockets.
  • Pokes persisted to a pokes table (powers free streaks / who-missed-you).
  • Model: profiles, rooms, room_members, pokes, blocks, usage_counters + RLS & Realtime authorization policies.
  • Custom-avatar distribution: a full 17-PNG bundle is ~68 KB → feasible via Storage; it's a lifecycle problem (scoping, discovery, cleanup), not size. Deferred to v2.1.

8 · Scope & phasing (ship thin first)

v2.0 the shippable core (~10–11 dev-days): shared default Room (invite code) · predefined-22 avatars (your own custom shown locally to you) · live presence (with reconnect/sleep-wake/stale-ghost) · pokes + micro-messages · free streaks / who-missed-you · hardened generation (--verify-jwt + per-uid caps) · poke overlay done right · anon-auth + Keychain persistence · mute/block + server-side poke rate-limit.

v2.1 fast-follow: cross-member custom-avatar sync (others see your made-up character) · extra/multiple Rooms (Pro) · cosmetics / Room themes · custom poke-phrase & emoji packs · magic-link cross-device merge.

9 · Risks

  • Cold-start / network effect → the pair wedge (a Room of 2 is enough) + dead-simple invite codes + seed the founder's crew + "invite 1 person" onboarding.
  • Presence is low-frequency (the graveyard's killer) → make the poke the daily loop; streaks reward reciprocity.
  • Creepy vs cute → category mood only, never app/URL/keys; "appear away"; framed as affection; no manager dashboards.
  • AI cost scales with users → free = predefined + 1 photo + 1 taste; volume = paid credits; server-side daily caps + --verify-jwt.
  • Poke spam / harassment → server-side rate-limit, mute/block/report, room-scoped from day one.
  • Reconnect / sleep-wake / stale ghosts → explicit handling or the Room feels broken.
  • macOS focus etiquette → non-activating overlay, never steals focus.
  • Graveyard risk → bet: cute desktop-pet hook + poke toy (not "presence") + working at n = 2 = differentiated & viral inside friend groups.

10 · Success metrics

  • Activation: % of installs that join a Room and exchange a poke in week 1.
  • Magic number: a Room with ≥2 mutually-poking members (reciprocity, not headcount).
  • Daily poke rate + poke-back rate; streak retention.
  • W2 retention of members in a reciprocating Room.
  • Pro / credit conversion via describe-to-create after the free taste.

11 · The freemium logic, in one paragraph

You install to poke your person — free forever, works with just two of you, with predefined avatars, presence, unlimited pokes, and streaks. You pay (once, $14.99) and buy credits the moment you want to conjure characters by describing them — the delightful, shareable thing that also costs us OpenAI money — or run extra Rooms. The paywall sits exactly on our real costs. No subscription.

What the reviewers said

Product / GTM — verdict: REVISE (4/10) → addressed in rev. 2

Stop selling "presence" (it killed the graveyard); lead with poke + pet. The freemium line was inverted — don't paywall the private room or describe-to-create. Kill the subscription → one-time + credits. Move streaks / who-missed-you to free. Strongly consider a pairs/couples (Locket-style) wedge to beat cold-start and low frequency.

Engineering — verdict: 6/10 (buildable) → blockers promoted to requirements

supabase-swift gives native Realtime Presence + Broadcast (no hand-rolled sockets). Sprite bundles are only ~68 KB, so custom-avatar sync is a storage-lifecycle problem, not a size one. Blockers: harden generation (--verify-jwt + per-uid caps), server-side poke gating + non-activating overlay, reconnect/sleep-wake/stale-ghost. Ship a thin v2.0; defer custom-avatar sync, multi-room, cosmetics.
